Current caesar_lbrc_nvcomp is correctness-only:
- It uses nvCOMP LZ4 per bitplane stream.
- It is slower than Zstd due to many tiny GPU launches and cudaMalloc/cudaFree calls.
- Small ERA5 result:
  Zstd:       CR 5.67239, LBRC time 0.294 s
  nvCOMP LZ4: CR 4.85052, LBRC time 1.061 s

Next implementation:
1. Refactor encodeBlock to produce packed bitplanes without compressing.
2. Collect all packed bitplanes across blocks into one vector/list.
3. Use one nvcompBatchedLZ4CompressAsync call for all streams.
4. Scatter compressed outputs back into block.streams.
5. Similarly batch decompress all streams in decode().
6. Then compare against Python and C++ Zstd across all datasets/error bounds.
